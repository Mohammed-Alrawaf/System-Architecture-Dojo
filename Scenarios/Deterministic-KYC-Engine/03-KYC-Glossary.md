# Scenario 03: Architecture Glossary — KYC Modernization & Deterministic Orchestration

This document explains the key concepts used in the Deterministic KYC Orchestration scenario.

It is designed to bridge the gap between:
- technical implementation  
- business understanding  
- regulatory and compliance thinking  

---

## 1. Core Architecture Concepts

### Orchestration Layer

**Executive View:**  
A central control system that manages how different steps of the KYC process happen.

**Technical View:**  
A workflow engine that coordinates multiple services (IDP, risk scoring, identity verification) and manages the lifecycle and state of each KYC request.

---

### API Facade Pattern

**Executive View:**  
A simplified interface that hides complexity of legacy systems.

**Technical View:**  
A wrapper layer that exposes legacy systems (e.g., SOAP services) as modern APIs without modifying the underlying system.

---

### State Management

**Executive View:**  
A tracking system that shows where a customer application is in the process.

**Technical View:**  
A centralized database that records the lifecycle of each KYC request (e.g., `PROCESSING`, `MANUAL_REVIEW`, `COMPLETED`).

---

## 2. KYC & Compliance Concepts

### KYC (Know Your Customer)

**Executive View:**  
A regulatory process used by financial institutions to verify customer identity and assess risk.

**Technical View:**  
A multi-step workflow involving document verification, identity validation, risk scoring, and compliance approval.

---

### Straight-Through Processing (STP)

**Executive View:**  
A process where customer applications are approved automatically without human involvement.

**Technical View:**  
A workflow path where applications that meet predefined criteria are automatically approved based on deterministic rules.

---

### Reason Codes

**Executive View:**  
Clear explanations for why a decision was made.

**Technical View:**  
Structured outputs generated by rule-based systems that explain the logic behind approval or rejection decisions.

---

### Compliance Dashboard (Single Pane of Glass)

**Executive View:**  
A unified interface where compliance teams review and approve applications.

**Technical View:**  
A centralized UI that aggregates data from multiple systems and allows users to make final decisions.

---

## 3. Data Processing Concepts

### Intelligent Document Processing (IDP)

**Executive View:**  
A tool that reads and extracts information from documents automatically.

**Technical View:**  
An AI-assisted system that uses OCR and machine learning to convert unstructured documents (PDFs, images) into structured data.

---

### Data Validation

**Executive View:**  
A quality check to ensure information is correct before making a decision.

**Technical View:**  
A set of rules that verify format, completeness, and consistency of extracted data before processing continues.

---

## 4. Risk & Decisioning Concepts

### Risk API

**Executive View:**  
A system that evaluates how risky a customer is.

**Technical View:**  
A real-time service that applies deterministic rules and scoring logic to assess customer risk and produce a risk score with reason codes.

---

### Deterministic Decisioning

**Executive View:**  
A decision process that always produces the same result given the same input.

**Technical View:**  
A rule-based system where decisions are driven by predefined logic, ensuring repeatability, explainability, and auditability.

---

### Non-Deterministic (AI-Based) Decisioning

**Executive View:**  
A decision process that may vary depending on patterns and probabilities.

**Technical View:**  
A system where outputs are generated using machine learning or probabilistic models, which may not always produce identical results for the same input.

---

## 5. Deployment & Control Concepts

### Shadow Mode

**Executive View:**  
A testing mode where a new system runs in the background without affecting real customers.

**Technical View:**  
A deployment approach where the new system processes real data but does not impact production outcomes, allowing comparison with the legacy system.

---

### Canary Release

**Executive View:**  
A gradual rollout of a new system to reduce risk.

**Technical View:**  
A deployment strategy where a small percentage of traffic is routed to the new system before full rollout.

---

### Manual Review (Human-in-the-Loop)

**Executive View:**  
A fallback process where humans make the final decision.

**Technical View:**  
A system design pattern where automated workflows escalate cases to human operators when predefined conditions are not met.

---

## 6. Why This Matters

This glossary ensures that:

- technical decisions are understandable to non-technical stakeholders  
- compliance teams can align with system behavior  
- engineering teams use consistent terminology  

> Clear definitions reduce risk, improve alignment, and support regulatory compliance.
